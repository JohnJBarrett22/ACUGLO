How Microneedling Works

Microneedling is a minimally-invasive cosmetic procedure that helps to improve the appearance of the skin. It does this by stimulating the production of collagen and elastin, which are two important proteins that keep the skin looking firm and elastic. Microneedling also helps to reduce the appearance of scars, fine lines, and wrinkles.

The procedure works by using a device that contains tiny needles. These needles are used to create small punctures in the top layer of skin. This triggers the body's natural healing process, which then begins to produce collagen and elastin. The new collagen and elastin help to improve the appearance of the skin.

Microneedling is also beneficial for hair growth. The procedure can help to stimulate the scalp, which can promote hair growth. Microneedling is a safe and effective way to improve the appearance of the skin and promote hair growth.

Microneedling FAQ

What is microneedling?

Microneedling is a minimally invasive cosmetic procedure that involves the use of fine needles to create tiny punctures in the skin. The purpose of this treatment is to stimulate the body's natural production of collagen and elastin, which can help reduce the appearance of wrinkles, fine lines, and acne scars. Additionally, microneedling can also improve the texture and tone of the skin.

Microneedling is usually performed using a device known as a derma roller, which consists of a small barrel with hundreds of tiny needles. The derma roller is rolled over the skin in order to create the punctures. Microneedling can also be performed using a device known as a dermapen, which is similar to a derma roller but has fewer needles.

Microneedling is a relatively safe and effective procedure with minimal downtime. Side effects may include temporary redness, swelling, and bruising. More serious side effects are rare but can include infection and scarring. Microneedling should be performed by a trained and licensed professional.

What are the benefits of microneedling?

Microneedling has a wide range of potential benefits, including reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, improving skin texture and tone, minimizing pore size, and helping to fade acne scars. In addition, microneedling can be used to improve the absorption of topically applied products and can be used in conjunction with other modalities such as facial cosmetic acupuncture.

Recent studies have shown that microneedling can also be used to treat hair loss. In one study, microneedling was found to increase hair density by 36% after 24 weeks of treatment. Another study showed that microneedling improved hair growth in women with alopecia by stimulating the production of new hair follicles.

What can microneedling help with?

Microneedling can be used to treat a variety of conditions, including acne scars, fine lines and wrinkles, stretch marks, dark spots, and uneven skin tone. It is also effective in reducing pore size, improving the appearance of cellulite, and stimulating hair growth.

Does microneedling grow hair?

Yes, microneedling can grow hair. This is because microneedling creates tiny wounds in the scalp that stimulate the production of collagen and other healing factors. When these factors are produced in greater quantities, they can promote hair growth. Additionally, microneedling can also improve the absorption of minoxidil, a medication that is used to treat hair loss.

One study found that microneedling was effective in treating alopecia areata, an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss. In the study, patients who underwent microneedling treatment every two weeks for six months experienced an average 62 percent increase in hair density. Additionally, another study found that microneedling was effective in treating androgenetic alopecia, or pattern hair loss. In the study, patients who underwent microneedling treatment every four weeks for three months experienced an average 30 percent increase in hair density.

Microneedling is a safe and effective treatment for hair loss. However, as with any treatment, there are potential side effects. The most common side effects of microneedling include scalp irritation, redness, and bruising. These side effects are usually mild and resolve within a few days.

What age should you start microneedling?

There is no definitive answer to the question of what age someone should start microneedling. The decision should be made based on a number of factors, including skin type, goals, and concerns.

Microneedling can be beneficial for all skin types, but those with certain conditions may see especially dramatic results. For example, microneedling can help improve the appearance of acne scars, fine lines, and wrinkles. It can also help reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation and improve skin texture.

How long does microneedling last?

While the results of a single treatment can last for several months, it is typically recommended that people receive multiple treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart in order to achieve the best results. After a series of treatments, the effects of microneedling can last for years.

Is microneedling painful?

The answer may depend on a few factors, including your pain tolerance, the type of microneedling you undergo, and the area being treated. For example, traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) understands that certain areas of the body are more sensitive than others. Generally speaking, the needles used in microneedling are very fine, so the pain is usually minimal. However, some people do report feeling a slight prickling sensation during the treatment. Afterward, the treated area may be slightly red and swollen, but this typically goes away within a few hours.

In short, microneedling is not typically painful, but some people may experience discomfort during the treatment. Talk to us about your concerns and be sure to let us know if you experience any pain during the procedure.

What are the risks of microneedling?

Although microneedling is generally safe, there are some risks associated with the procedure. These risks include infection, skin irritation, and scarring.

Infection is the most serious risk associated with microneedling. The needle punctures can provide a pathway for bacteria to enter the skin. This can lead to local skin infections or even systemic infections if the bacteria spread through the bloodstream.

Skin irritation is another common side effect of microneedling. The needles can cause redness, swelling, and bruising. These side effects are usually temporary and resolve on their own within a few days.

Scarring is a more rare but potential complication of microneedling. The needles can damage the skin and create permanent scars. This risk can be minimized by avoiding deep needle penetration and using sterile needles.

Overall, microneedling is a safe and effective procedure with minimal risks. The most serious complication is infection, but this can be avoided by taking proper precautions. Skin irritation and scarring are also possible, but rare complications. Microneedling is an excellent option for those looking to improve the appearance of their skin with minimal risks.

How long after microneedling can I wash my face?

Immediately after your microneedling treatment, your skin will be sensitive and vulnerable to irritants. It is important to wait at least 12 hours before washing your face with a gentle cleanser. You should also avoid using harsh chemicals or exfoliants for at least 24 hours after treatment. If you have any redness or swelling, you can apply a cool compress to the affected area.

It is normal to experience some redness and swelling after microneedling. These side effects usually resolve within 24 hours. However, it is important to avoid touching or scratching the treated area, as this could lead to infection. If you have any concerns, be sure to speak with us.
